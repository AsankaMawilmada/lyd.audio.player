Privacy Policy

Lyd is a music player that works entirely with the files already on your phone. This page explains, plainly, what that means for your data.

In short

  • Lyd has no internet access — it cannot send anything anywhere, because it has no way to.
  • We do not collect, see, or store your personal information on any server, because we don't run one.
  • No accounts, no analytics, no ads, no crash reporting, no third-party SDKs of any kind.
  • Everything Lyd creates — playlists, favorites, cached artwork — stays in Lyd's own storage on your device.

01 Introduction

Lyd ("the app", "we", "our") is developed by Inoaspect. Lyd scans and plays the audio files already stored on your device — it is not a streaming service, does not require sign-in, and does not have a backend server. This policy describes what that architecture means in practice for any personal information.

By using Lyd, you agree to the practices (or, more precisely, the absence of practices) described below.

02 Information we don't collect

Lyd does not collect, transmit, or have access to, any of the following:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or any other account or contact information — Lyd has no sign-in of any kind.
  • Your location.
  • Analytics or usage statistics (what you play, how often you open the app, how long you listen).
  • Crash reports or diagnostic logs sent off your device.
  • Advertising identifiers — Lyd shows no ads.
  • The contents, titles, or metadata of your music library, sent anywhere off your phone.

This isn't a policy choice layered on top of a data-collecting app — Lyd does not request the INTERNET permission at all, so there is no network path for any of the above to travel over, even in principle.

03 Permissions we use, and why

Lyd asks for a small number of on-device permissions, each tied to a specific feature:

PermissionWhat it's for
Music & audio / Storage
(READ_MEDIA_AUDIO, READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE)		 Lets Lyd find and read the audio files on your device so it can build your library. Reading only — Lyd doesn't modify your files.
Storage (legacy)
(WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE, Android 9 and below only)		 Only used if you choose to delete a song from within Lyd, on older Android versions that require it. Newer Android versions handle this through a system confirmation dialog instead.
Notifications
(POST_NOTIFICATIONS)		 Shows the playback notification with play/pause/skip controls, including on the lock screen.
Foreground service, wake lock
(FOREGROUND_SERVICE, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MEDIA_PLAYBACK, WAKE_LOCK)		 Lets music keep playing when Lyd is in the background or your screen is off.

04 What Lyd stores on your device

To do its job, Lyd keeps a small amount of data — all of it local, in storage private to the app, none of it visible to us:

  • Playlists and Favorites — the lists you create, stored as references to your existing files.
  • Recently played — a short local history (most-recent 20 tracks) used to populate the "Recently Played" row.
  • Cached album artwork — artwork already embedded in your files or supplied by your device's media library, cached locally so Lyd doesn't have to re-read it every time.
  • Equalizer and playback settings — your chosen preset, band levels, and similar preferences.

This data lives in Lyd's app-private storage. It is not synced, backed up to any Lyd-controlled server, or shared with anyone, because there is nowhere for it to go.

05 Lyrics

Lyd can display lyrics for a track, but only ever by reading files already on your device: a matching .lrc file stored alongside the song, or lyrics already embedded in the song's own tags. Lyd does not query any lyrics website or API, so no track or artist information is ever sent anywhere to fetch lyrics.

06 Third-party services

Lyd contains no third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs, and makes no network requests of any kind. There is no third-party service to disclose data practices for, because none are integrated into the app.

07 Children's privacy

Because Lyd does not collect personal information from any user, it does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Lyd is suitable for a general audience on that basis.

08 Data retention & deletion

All data Lyd stores lives in the app's private storage on your device. You can remove it at any time by:

  • Clearing Lyd's app storage from Android's Settings → Apps → Lyd → Storage, or
  • Uninstalling Lyd, which removes all app-private data as part of the standard Android uninstall process.

Because none of this data ever reaches us, there is nothing for us to separately retain or delete on your behalf.

09 Changes to this policy

If Lyd's data practices ever change — for example, if a future version adds an optional online feature — this page will be updated first, with a new effective date above, before that version is released.
